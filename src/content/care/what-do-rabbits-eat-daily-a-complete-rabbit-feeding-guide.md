# Understanding a Rabbit's Natural Diet

Rabbits are natural grazers, meaning they are designed to eat small amounts continuously throughout the day. In the wild, rabbits mainly survive on grasses, plants, and leafy vegetation. Domestic rabbits still require a diet that closely matches this natural feeding behaviour.

A healthy rabbit diet helps support:

- Digestion
- Dental health
- Weight management
- Energy levels
- Overall lifespan

Feeding the wrong foods, or too many treats, can quickly lead to serious health problems.

## Hay Should Make Up Most of a Rabbit's Diet

Fresh hay is the single most important part of a rabbit's diet and should make up around 80–90% of what they eat each day.

Hay helps:

- Keep digestion moving properly
- Prevent dangerous gut problems
- Wear down constantly growing teeth
- Encourage natural foraging behaviour

Rabbits should always have unlimited access to fresh hay at all times.

### Best Types of Hay for Rabbits

Popular healthy options include:

- Timothy hay
- Meadow hay
- Orchard grass hay
- Botanical hay

Hay should smell fresh, look dry, and be free from dust or mould.

## Fresh Greens and Vegetables

Fresh leafy greens provide extra nutrients and variety in a rabbit's diet.

Good daily vegetables include:

- Romaine lettuce
- Coriander
- Basil
- Mint
- Rocket
- Spring greens
- Parsley

Introduce new foods slowly to avoid upsetting your rabbit's stomach.

### Vegetables to Feed in Moderation

Some vegetables are safe in small amounts but should not be overfed:

- Carrots
- Kale
- Spinach
- Broccoli

These are better treated as occasional additions rather than daily staples.

## Rabbit Pellets

Pellets can provide additional nutrients, but they should only form a small part of an adult rabbit's diet.

Choose:

- High-fibre pellets
- Plain pellets rather than colourful mixes

Avoid muesli-style rabbit food, as rabbits often pick out unhealthy pieces and leave the nutritious parts behind.

### How Much Pellet Food Should Rabbits Eat?

The exact amount depends on:

- Age
- Weight
- Activity level
- Breed

In general, adult rabbits only need a small measured portion each day alongside unlimited hay.

## Fresh Water Is Essential

Rabbits must always have access to clean fresh water.

Many rabbits prefer water bowls over bottles because they are easier and more natural to drink from. Bowls should be cleaned and refilled daily.

## Foods Rabbits Should Never Eat

Some foods are dangerous or toxic to rabbits. Avoid feeding:

- Chocolate
- Onions
- Garlic
- Avocado
- Iceberg lettuce
- Bread
- Dairy products
- Sugary snacks
- Processed human food

Sudden dietary changes can also upset a rabbit's digestive system.

## Feeding Baby Rabbits

Young rabbits have slightly different dietary needs than adults.

Baby rabbits:

- Need unlimited hay
- Usually receive more pellets while growing
- Should have vegetables introduced gradually

Any major dietary changes should be made slowly over time.

## Signs of a Healthy Rabbit Diet

A rabbit eating a healthy diet will usually:

- Produce regular droppings
- Maintain a healthy weight
- Stay active and alert
- Have good teeth condition
- Eat hay consistently throughout the day

A sudden loss of appetite in rabbits can become serious very quickly and may require veterinary attention.

## Creating a Simple Daily Feeding Routine

A basic daily rabbit feeding routine may look like:

**Morning**

- Fresh hay top-up
- Measured pellets
- Fresh water

**Evening**

- Leafy greens
- More hay
- Water refresh

Hay should always remain available between meals.

## Final Thoughts

A healthy rabbit diet is simple once the basics are understood. Unlimited hay, fresh greens, clean water, and controlled pellet portions form the foundation of good rabbit nutrition. Feeding the right foods every day helps rabbits stay healthy, active, and happy throughout their lives.
